# Maven Analytics Space Challenge 
- **Short Description:**  
  This dashboard provides an analytical view of worldwide space missions from 1957 to 2022, highlighting mission trends, success rates, and performance by country, company, and rocket type. The aim is to uncover insights into technological growth, private sector emergence, and the overall evolution of space travel.

- **Tech Stack:**  
  Power BI • DAX • Excel • Data Modeling • Power Query

- **Data Source:**  
  Dataset sourced from **Maven Analytics – Space Missions Challenge**. The dataset includes mission details such as launch year, rocket, company, country, mission status, and cost.

- **Features and Highlights:**

  - **Business Problem:**  
    Understanding global trends and performance in space exploration, comparing public vs private contributions, and identifying how mission success evolved over time.

  - **Goal of the Dashboard:**  
    To visualize key milestones in the history of space travel, identify top-performing companies and rockets, and assess the success and activity rate across decades.

  - **Key Visuals:**  
    - *Area Charts:* Show mission trends and company growth over the decades.  
    - *Donut Charts:* Compare overall mission success rates and rocket activity (active vs retired).  
    - *Matrix Table:* Displays company success consistency across decades.  
    - *Bar & Treemap Visuals:* Highlight leading countries and rockets based on mission count.  
    - *Column Chart:* Shows launch frequency patterns by weekday.  
    - *KPI Cards:* Summarize overall missions, success rate, companies, rockets, and launch sites.

  - **Business Impact and Insights:**  
    - Space missions surged again post-2010, driven by private companies like SpaceX and CASC.  
    - Global success rate stands at ~90%, reflecting significant advancements in rocket reliability.  
    - USA, Russia, and China remain top contributors, while emerging players are rapidly growing.  
    - The 2010–2022 era shows commercialization dominating the space industry.  
    - Rockets like Falcon 9, Long March 2C, and Soyuz demonstrate exceptional success and longevity.
